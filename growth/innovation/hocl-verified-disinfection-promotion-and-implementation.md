# UCM HOCl Verified Disinfection Pilot

## Decision

UCM should develop hypochlorous acid (HOCl) as a controlled, evidence-led disinfection service, not promote it as a miracle product.

The commercially credible proposition is:

> UCM cleans first, disinfects eligible surfaces with a product approved for the intended use, verifies the process and gives the client a clear completion record.

The differentiator is the complete system: correct cleaning, product selection, measured concentration, labelled contact time, before-and-after verification, trained application and documented proof.

This remains a pilot until UCM selects the exact product or in-situ system and verifies its UK regulatory status, label, Safety Data Sheet (SDS), efficacy dossier, permitted surfaces, application methods and supplier evidence.

## Important Truths Before Promotion

- HOCl means hypochlorous acid. It is a weak acid. A formulated HOCl solution may be close to neutral pH, but UCM must only state the measured range shown in the selected product specification.
- HOCl is a chemical substance. UCM must not call it chemical-free.
- The active form and efficacy of chlorine solutions depend on pH, concentration, contact time, organic loading, surface condition and the target organism.
- Cleaning and disinfection are different. Visible dirt and organic material should be removed before disinfectant is applied unless the product label and evidence explicitly support a combined process.
- ATP testing measures biological residue and cleaning performance. It is not directly equivalent to microbiological testing and does not prove that a named pathogen was killed.
- A clean ATP result does not prove sterility, hospital operating-theatre equivalence or compliance with a healthcare environmental standard.
- Surface mould treatment does not fix the cause of mould. Moisture, leaks, ventilation, condensation and damaged materials must also be assessed and addressed.
- Fogging creates an inhalation exposure route and is not the default application method. It requires product-specific permission, a COSHH assessment, controlled exclusion, ventilation and re-entry rules.
- A UK supplier or on-site generation model may reduce packaging and transport, but carbon savings are not automatic. They must be measured against a defined alternative.

## Before-and-After Test Protocol

### Purpose

The test demonstrates whether the agreed cleaning process reduced detectable organic residue at defined surfaces. It does not by itself prove disinfection efficacy against a particular bacterium, virus, fungus or spore.

### Standard ATP Method

1. Select fixed, high-touch test points such as a door plate, desk edge, washroom touchpoint or shared control panel.
2. Record the site, room, surface material, exact test location, date, time, operator, ATP meter and swab batch.
3. Use a reusable location template, for example a consistent 10 cm by 10 cm area where the surface permits.
4. Take the pre-clean swab before UCM starts work.
5. Photograph the test point without capturing personal or confidential information.
6. Complete physical cleaning and the labelled disinfection process.
7. Respect the full product contact and drying requirements before the post-process swab.
8. Swab the same defined area using the same technique and instrument.
9. Record both relative light unit (RLU) readings and the percentage change.
10. If the post-clean result fails the site-specific threshold, re-clean, investigate and repeat once. Record the failure rather than hiding it.

### Threshold Rule

UCM must not advertise an absolute target such as "under 10" until the exact ATP instrument, surface type, method, manufacturer guidance and site baseline have been agreed. RLU values are instrument- and method-dependent.

The initial pilot should report:

- Before RLU.
- After RLU.
- Percentage change.
- Pass, investigate or re-clean against a documented site-specific threshold.
- Product concentration, application method and contact time.
- Any limitation or exception.

### When Microbiological Evidence Is Required

If a client requires evidence about viable organisms or a named organism, UCM should commission an appropriately accredited laboratory and agree the sampling method, controls, chain of custody and interpretation before work begins. UCM must not translate an ATP result into a pathogen-kill claim.

## Product and Supplier Gate

No purchase, pilot or promotion begins until the operations and health-and-safety owners hold and approve:

- Exact product and manufacturer name.
- UK supplier identity and supply-chain evidence.
- Current label and SDS.
- GB and, if relevant, Northern Ireland regulatory status.
- Authorisation or approval number where required.
- Active substance and product type, normally PT2 for eligible non-food-contact surface uses.
- Approved professional-user category and intended uses.
- Supported organisms and efficacy standards at the marketed concentration and contact time.
- Permitted application methods, including whether spraying or fogging is supported.
- Surface compatibility, corrosion and fabric-colour evidence.
- Food-contact, toy, rinse and residue instructions where relevant.
- Shelf life, storage conditions and concentration-monitoring method.
- Batch traceability and certificate-of-analysis process.
- Manufacturer training and incident guidance.
- Mixing incompatibilities and emergency arrangements.
- Evidence for every pH, safety, environmental, logistics or carbon claim.

If UCM generates active chlorine at the point of use, it must separately verify the HSE rules for in-situ generation, the precursor, device, active-substance status and the generated product's permitted use.

## Application Rules

### Routine Hard Surfaces

Use on eligible clean, compatible surfaces only where the selected label permits. Record concentration and contact time. Do not mix HOCl with another cleaner or disinfectant unless the manufacturer explicitly authorises the process.

### Toys and Child-Contact Surfaces

Do not use the phrase "safe for children's toys" by default. Only treat toys or child-contact surfaces if the exact product label permits the surface and use, and follow any rinse, drying, exclusion and re-entry requirements. Children must not be present during uncontrolled spraying or fogging.

### Toilets and Washrooms

Use as part of a defined two-stage cleaning and disinfection process where the label supports the surfaces and target claims. HOCl does not replace scale removal, physical soil removal or correct washroom equipment separation.

### Mould

UCM may only claim fungicidal or yeasticidal performance that appears in the selected product's supported efficacy dossier and label, at the stated concentration and contact time.

For mould work:

1. Document the visible area and moisture indicators.
2. Identify or escalate the underlying damp, leak, ventilation or condensation cause.
3. Use trained personnel and suitable containment/PPE for the assessed risk.
4. Remove or escalate porous materials that cannot be effectively remediated.
5. Treat eligible hard surfaces using the approved method.
6. Dry the area and revisit after the agreed period.

UCM must never promise that surface treatment alone prevents mould returning.

### Fogging

Fogging is a controlled future application, not the launch offer. It can proceed only when:

- The exact product label and efficacy evidence support the delivery method and intended use.
- The site-specific COSHH assessment addresses aerosol inhalation.
- The area is unoccupied and secured against entry.
- Sensitive materials, alarms, electronics, food and ventilation systems have been assessed.
- Droplet size, dose, distribution and contact time are validated.
- Ventilation and re-entry criteria are written and followed.
- The client approves the method and receives a completion record.

Manual wipe or controlled low-aerosol application should remain the default during the pilot.

## Carbon and Logistics Position

The opportunity is credible but unproven. A UK-made or correctly managed in-situ product could reduce transported water, long-distance logistics, packaging and stock waste compared with a defined incumbent product. It also consumes electricity and uses precursors, packaging, equipment and local transport.

UCM should build a carbon evidence ledger with:

- Comparator product and functional unit, such as one treated square metre or one completed site visit.
- Product or precursor quantity used.
- Product concentration and waste.
- Packaging mass and disposal route.
- Supplier origin and delivery distance.
- UCM delivery mileage attributable to the service.
- Electricity and water used for in-situ generation, where applicable.
- Equipment life and maintenance assumptions.
- Current UK greenhouse-gas conversion factors.
- Calculation boundary, assumptions, owner and review date.

Until that ledger is complete, permitted wording is:

> UCM is evaluating UK supply and lower-logistics disinfection options. Any carbon benefit will be measured against the product and process it replaces before we make a quantified claim.

Do not say "zero carbon," "carbon neutral," "planet safe," "environmentally friendly," "new carbon margin" or "cuts your carbon footprint" without a complete and supportable calculation.

## 30-Day Pilot

### Week 1: Evidence Gate

- Shortlist no more than three UK product or in-situ system options.
- Obtain the full supplier dossier and check HSE status.
- Select one exact use case and exclude regulated/high-risk environments.
- Complete COSHH, method, compatibility and training drafts.
- Cost the complete service.

### Week 2: Controlled Internal Trial

- Train two named UCM operators.
- Map five repeatable test surfaces.
- Test application, contact time, concentration and documentation.
- Record failures, material effects, odour, workflow time and waste.

### Week 3: Design-Partner Trial

- Run the process at one consenting low-risk client site.
- Collect before/after ATP data without making microbial claims.
- Issue a manual Proof Passport.
- Ask the buyer whether the evidence is useful and what they would pay for it.

### Week 4: Commercial Decision

- Review safety, results, labour time, repeatability, client value and gross margin.
- Approve, redesign or stop the service.
- Only then release product-specific promotional content.

## Pilot KPIs

- Percentage of test points passing the agreed post-clean threshold.
- Re-clean and investigation rate.
- Average before-to-after RLU change, segmented by surface type.
- Percentage of visits with complete concentration/contact-time records.
- Operator time and consumable cost per visit.
- Client willingness to pay and conversion to a recurring add-on.
- Gross margin after testing and rework.
- Complaints, material damage, exposure incidents and near misses.
- Packaging, delivery, product, water and electricity activity data captured.

Any safety incident, unsupported supplier claim, unauthorised use, repeated material incompatibility or inability to verify concentration is an immediate stop condition.
