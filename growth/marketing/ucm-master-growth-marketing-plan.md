# UCM Growth System, Channel Plans and Innovation Portfolio

## Executive Summary

UCM is not simply a cleaning website. The growth system has three connected parts:

1. A public website and customer/client hub that makes UCM easy to trust and buy from.
2. An internal sales, operations, targeting and campaign center.
3. A Media Engine that turns real work into approved videos, social content, campaigns and measurable learning.

The objective is not to publish for appearance. UCM must create recurring service products, generate qualified enquiries, prove delivery and connect marketing activity to contract revenue and margin.

## Commercial Position

UCM should be positioned as a London cleaning, maintenance and property-care partner for commercial buyers, property managers, landlords, residents and specialist technical environments.

The strongest strategic lane is:

> Cleaning plus responsive maintenance for London property and workplace buyers.

The company story supports this position:

- Founded in 1994 as Universal Technical Cleaning.
- Historic technical-cleaning experience, including an existing website reference to Morgan Stanley that requires approval before wider campaign use.
- Commercial, residential and specialist cleaning.
- Responsive maintenance, property improvements and renovation support.
- A family-run operating story with a credible founder voice.

UCM must correct inconsistent age claims on the live website and use one verified founding statement everywhere.

## Brutally Honest Gaps

- The website is a polished brochure rather than a customer platform.
- The case-studies page does not prove the level of experience claimed elsewhere.
- Valuable historic credibility is buried instead of converted into approved case studies.
- Service offers are broad and not packaged by buyer, outcome, service level or commercial value.
- The quote form lacks postcode, property type, frequency, urgency, access and buyer qualification.
- Customers cannot manage quotes, bookings, evidence, documents, issues or recurring work.
- UCM has no connected CRM, quote pipeline, renewal system or customer-value reporting in this repository.
- The current Media Center is a static prototype.
- The separate Media Engine still contains mocked watching, transcription, platform publishing and metrics paths.
- There is no reliable link between a post, an enquiry, a quote, a contract and gross margin.
- Private media repositories contain committed environment files that must be treated as exposed and rotated separately.

## Customer Hub Blueprint

The future customer experience must support separate journeys for homeowners, landlords, office managers, property managers and commercial clients.

Core capabilities:

- Service and postcode availability.
- Guided quote intake with approved photo/video upload.
- Human-reviewed AI scope assistance.
- Quote, booking, invoice and document history.
- Job status, service notifications and recurring booking management.
- Before/after evidence and completed-service checklists.
- Support tickets, maintenance requests and complaint resolution.
- Multi-site dashboards for commercial and property-management clients.
- Insurance, accreditation, RAMS and compliance access.
- Review requests, rebooking, upgrades and cleaning-plus-maintenance cross-sell.
- A visible human support route throughout.

## Internal Growth and Operations Center

The internal center must eventually provide:

- Lead intake from web, phone, Meta, Google, social, referrals and partners.
- CRM stages: enquiry, qualified, site visit, quote, won, lost, active and renewal.
- Quote-response timers and follow-up tasks.
- Customer, site, access, service, frequency and contract records.
- Scheduling, route grouping, capacity and team assignment.
- Quality inspections, service evidence, issues and satisfaction.
- Revenue, margin, acquisition cost, conversion, retention and lifetime value.
- London opportunity mapping connected to real UCM outcomes.
- Reporting by channel, borough, service, hook, CTA and contract value.

## Media Engine Role

The Media Engine stays behind the customer experience:

`Job media -> consent check -> transcription and tagging -> script and clip suggestions -> UCM branding -> human approval -> publishing -> lead attribution -> learning`

Every asset must record service, postcode, borough, buyer, proof status, consent, campaign, hook, CTA, landing page and approval state.

## AI-Assisted Economical Service

Approved core position:

> UCM uses AI-assisted planning to reduce administrative waste, improve scheduling, produce clearer scopes and respond with more efficient quotes. Where those efficiencies reduce operating costs, UCM aims to pass that value to customers without lowering service standards.

AI does not clean the site and does not make final service decisions without people. UCM must not claim guaranteed savings, lowest prices or invented percentages. Quantified claims require evidence from the AI Efficiency Ledger.

## Commercial Measurement

Track business outcomes rather than vanity metrics:

- Quote-response time and administrative time per quote.
- Travel and route efficiency.
- Rework, complaints and first-time resolution.
- Leads and qualified leads by source.
- Quote-to-contract conversion.
- Revenue and gross margin by service.
- Contract value, recurring revenue, retention and renewal.
- Content-assisted pipeline and revenue.
- Proof Passport usage and customer satisfaction.
- AI-efficiency evidence and claims readiness.

## Governance

- Human review is mandatory for AI-generated scopes and recommendations.
- AI use must be explained clearly when it materially affects the customer.
- Environmental claims require specific evidence and must comply with the CMA Green Claims Code.
- No customer, logo, prestigious-client reference, review or site media may publish without approval.
- No automated connection spam, fake reviews, unsupported guarantees or invented savings.
- The claims register is the source of truth for approved and blocked wording.
