## 4. Risk Rating

Use UCM's approved scoring method. A simple starting method is:

- Likelihood: 1 rare, 2 unlikely, 3 possible, 4 likely, 5 almost certain.
- Severity: 1 minor, 2 moderate, 3 serious, 4 major, 5 fatal or life-changing.
- Risk score: likelihood multiplied by severity.

The approving competent person must define acceptable thresholds and additional controls. Do not accept a score merely because a number appears low.

## 7. Method Statement

Adapt the sequence to the actual job.

1. Supervisor confirms the approved scope, team, site contact and RAMS version.
2. Team signs in and completes the site induction or local briefing.
3. Supervisor checks access, welfare, emergency arrangements, products, equipment and PPE.
4. Work area is isolated or signed where required to protect occupants and the public.
5. Products are prepared and used only in accordance with the product-specific COSHH assessment and manufacturer instructions.
6. Work proceeds in the agreed sequence: `[INSERT SITE-SPECIFIC SEQUENCE]`.
7. Waste is separated, handled and disposed of through the agreed route.
8. Operative completes the service checklist and reports defects, damage, spills, exposure or unexpected hazards.
9. Supervisor inspects the work, records corrective actions and confirms the area is safe to return.
10. Team signs out; incidents, near misses and lessons are escalated and recorded.
