# UCM One Login, Central Digital Platform and Supplier Evidence Pack

> **Current starting point:** Use the [UCM Level 1 Master Procurement Register](ucm-level-1-master-procurement-register.md) for the 12 basic items requested from Carlos and the first GBP 50,000-and-under opportunities. This document is the advanced evidence library for later frameworks, major buyers and contract-specific requirements.

## Purpose

This is the master list UCM should build once and reuse across councils, housing providers, property managers, developers, FM primes and public frameworks.

It separates four different jobs that are easily confused:

1. **GOV.UK One Login** identifies an individual user.
2. **Find a Tender's Central Digital Platform (CDP)** records UCM's core supplier information and issues its Procurement Organisation Identifier (PPON).
3. **Buyer e-tender portals** register UCM on each authority or company's own system.
4. **Tender submissions** add contract-specific commercial, technical and social-value responses.

Registering on one system does not register UCM everywhere and does not make UCM an approved supplier. A portal account is access to an opportunity, not revenue.

## Official Registration Sequence

### 1. Agree the administrators

- Name one senior first administrator.
- Name a backup administrator.
- Each person uses their own work email and their own GOV.UK One Login.
- Never share a One Login account or two-factor authentication code.
- Use a monitored shared UCM organisation email when the supplier record asks for the company's email.

The first administrator can maintain the organisation record and approve other users. Follow the [official three-step registration guide](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/procurement-act-2023-short-guides/suppliers-how-to-register-your-organisation-and-first-administrator-on-find-a-tender-in-three-easy-steps-html).

### 2. Create GOV.UK One Login

The named administrator needs:

- Individual work email.
- Access to the email verification code.
- Strong unique password.
- Authenticator app or mobile number for two-factor authentication.
- Secure recovery arrangements.

### 3. Register UCM on Find a Tender

Choose the **Supplier** role and use UCM's exact legal identity:

- Universal Cleaning & Maintenance Services Ltd.
- Companies House number `12265169`.
- Registered and postal addresses.
- Shared organisation email.
- Website.
- VAT status and number.
- Organisation type and legal form.
- Governing law and incorporation date.
- SME classification.

Record the PPON exactly as issued. Do not invent it or confuse it with the Companies House number, D-U-N-S number or an old Supplier Registration Service identifier.

### 4. Complete the six supplier-information sections

The current platform asks for:

1. Basic information.
2. Connected persons.
3. Qualifications.
4. Trade assurances.
5. Exclusions.
6. Financial information.

The [official detailed walkthrough](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/procurement-act-2023-short-guides/suppliers-detailed-walkthrough-how-an-administrator-completes-and-updates-supplier-information-html) should be followed while entering data.

### 5. Declare and share

An authorised person confirms the information is accurate and supplies their name, job title, work email and postal address. Generate a current share code or download the supplier-information file for the buyer.

UCM controls who receives this information, but UCM remains responsible for its accuracy. The CDP does not validate the truth of the supplier record. See the [Central Digital Platform factsheet](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/procurement-act-2023-short-guides/central-digital-platform-factsheet-html).

## Documents and Evidence UCM Must Produce

### A. Legal identity and registration

- Certificate of incorporation.
- Current Companies House extract.
- Legal-name and trading-name reconciliation.
- Registered and postal address confirmation.
- VAT certificate or non-registration declaration.
- Website and shared company contact email.
- PPON after CDP registration.
- D-U-N-S number only where a buyer or platform requires it.
- Authorised-signatory and delegated-authority record.
- Current connected-person register.
- Qualification register.
- Trade-assurance and accreditation register.
- Exclusion-ground review for UCM, connected persons and relied-on subcontractors or consortium members.
- Current CDP share code or supplier-information file.

Connected-person data can include dates of birth, nationalities and addresses. Store it only in private controlled systems.

### B. Financial and payment evidence

- Two to three years of full accounts.
- Current management accounts.
- Cash-flow and turnover forecast where requested.
- Accountant contact and supporting explanation.
- Completed buyer or framework financial viability assessment.
- Explanation of micro-entity accounts and current trading capacity.
- Largest safe contract and mobilisation-capacity statement.
- Verified company bank evidence supplied only through secure onboarding.
- UTR and PAYE details only where legitimately requested.
- Parent-company, guarantor, consortium or subcontractor financial evidence where relied on.

Micro accounts may satisfy filing law but can still be too thin for a buyer's financial-capacity test. The accountant should help UCM present a defensible current position.

### C. Insurance

- Employers' liability schedule.
- Public liability schedule.
- Professional indemnity schedule where advice, scope design or technical work is offered.
- Business motor or fleet cover.
- Products and treatment-risk cover for disinfection or specialist treatments.
- Pollution or environmental cover where the contract requires it.
- Broker confirmation for any required increase.
- Insurance expiry register and renewal owner.

For RM6264, the current pack requires at least:

- Employers' liability: `GBP 5m`.
- Public liability: `GBP 1m`.
- Professional indemnity: `GBP 1m`.

Every buyer's current requirement must still be checked. Do not assume one framework's limits apply everywhere.

### D. Health, safety and cleaning operations

- Signed health and safety policy.
- Site-survey process.
- Risk-assessment and method-statement templates.
- Genuine site-specific RAMS examples.
- COSHH procedure.
- Product inventory, current safety data sheets and COSHH assessments.
- Dilution, labelling, storage and substitution controls.
- PPE matrix and issue records.
- Training and competency matrix.
- Manual-handling, slips/trips, lone-working and equipment controls.
- Working-at-height, electrical-area, sharps, biohazard and PUWER controls where applicable.
- Equipment, maintenance, service and PAT register where relevant.
- Accident, near-miss, RIDDOR and enforcement history.
- Incident escalation and corrective-action process.
- Quality inspections, non-conformance and improvement process.
- Complaints and service-recovery procedure.
- Business-continuity and emergency-response plan.
- Product and consumables supply-continuity plan.
- Subcontractor approval, induction, supervision and monitoring.
- Waste duty-of-care process, waste-carrier evidence and transfer notes where applicable.
- Site security, keys, access codes and photography controls.

### E. HOCl and disinfection gate

Before UCM makes a disinfecting, mould, fogging or carbon claim, retain:

- UK product manufacturer and supplier evidence.
- Applicable regulatory status and authorised use.
- Current safety data sheet.
- Product-specific COSHH assessment.
- Staff training and competency.
- Verified concentration, application method and contact time.
- Surface/material compatibility.
- Independent efficacy evidence for the exact product and use.
- Claim register showing wording that is approved, blocked or requires evidence.
- Insurance confirmation for the treatment.
- Before/after testing protocol that explains the test method and its limitations.

Do not market "hospital operating theatre clean", "100 times stronger than bleach", "kills all fungi" or similar absolutes without product-specific, use-specific and legally reviewed evidence.

### F. Workforce and employment

- Right-to-work checking procedure.
- Employment contracts, PAYE, pension, holiday and payroll controls.
- National Living Wage and London Living Wage position.
- Recruitment, induction and probation process.
- Role-based training and refresher records.
- Identity, uniform and site-access controls.
- Lawful DBS and vetting rules for relevant roles.
- Safeguarding policy for vulnerable-person settings.
- Equality, diversity and inclusion policy.
- Anti-harassment policy.
- Modern-slavery controls and labour-supply-chain checks.
- Whistleblowing, grievance and disciplinary procedures.
- Staff-transfer and TUPE capability with professional support.
- Recruitment, absence-cover and mobilisation plan.

### G. Governance, information and cyber security

- Data-protection policy.
- ICO registration or documented exemption assessment.
- Privacy notices for employees, customers and website users.
- Data-processing terms and subprocessor register.
- Retention and deletion schedule.
- Data-breach and incident-response plan.
- Named user accounts, MFA and leaver-access removal.
- Password-manager and device-security controls.
- Encrypted backup and tested restore process.
- Current Cyber Essentials certificate where required.
- Anti-bribery, fraud, gifts and hospitality policy.
- Conflict-of-interest declaration and register.
- Confidentiality and secure-site handling procedure.
- Supplier code and buyer-code acceptance control.

RM6264 currently requires Cyber Essentials for appointment. Other buyers may ask for different or additional controls.

### H. Environment and social value

- Signed environmental policy.
- Defined carbon-reporting boundary and measured baseline.
- Carbon-reduction plan with owners, dates and evidence.
- Route, mileage and scheduling measurement.
- Product concentration, dosing, water and packaging evidence.
- Waste, reuse and recycling evidence.
- Sustainable procurement policy.
- Social-value response library.
- Costed and measurable local-employment and training commitments.
- Contract-level social-value evidence ledger.
- Equality and public-sector-equality response.

Do not invent carbon savings. Calculate them from verified product, transport, energy, waste and operational data.

### I. Commercial bid library

- One-page UCM capability statement.
- Buyer-specific company profile.
- Service catalogue and service boundaries.
- Geographic coverage and response hours.
- Approved case studies.
- Approved reference register.
- Organisation chart.
- Key-person CVs.
- Method-statement answer bank.
- Mobilisation and transition plan.
- Staffing, supervision and resilience plan.
- TUPE assumptions and support route.
- Quality, KPI and monthly-reporting example.
- Proof Passport example.
- Complaints, escalation and governance model.
- Business-continuity answer.
- Social-value answer library.
- Carbon and sustainability answer library.
- Pricing model, rate card and commercial assumptions.
- Labour, equipment, consumables, travel, overhead, inflation, risk and margin model.
- Contract-specific risk register.
- Clarification and deviation register.
- Tender declarations and sign-off record.
- Bid/no-bid scorecard.

### J. Media and reputation proof

- Customer and site-media consent record.
- Client-name and logo permission.
- Approved reviews and testimonials.
- Before-and-after evidence with date, site and scope.
- Genuine service-performance figures.
- Google Business Profile and review-request process.
- Brand assets and current contact details.
- Evidence that every marketing claim matches the bid response.

## Buyer-Specific Appendices

| Buyer route | Add to the universal pack |
|---|---|
| Council or public body | PPON, current CDP share code/file, procurement-specific questionnaire, FVRA, FOI confidentiality schedule, TUPE position and authority-specific social value |
| Housing provider | Resident communication, vulnerable-resident controls, safeguarding, communal/void methods, complaints and local evidence |
| FM prime | Supplier code, flow-down terms, subcontract insurance, enterprise reporting, accreditations and service-capacity statement |
| Property manager | Communal-area inspections, resident experience, rapid response, portfolio reporting and named-site references |
| Developer / new build | Handover cleaning, snag observations, sequencing, site access, programme capacity and evidence pack |
| Data centre / technical site | Vetting, shutdown and electrical controls, secure access, technical methods, supervision and incident response |
| Health setting | Only bid services UCM can evidence. Define non-clinical boundaries and do not imply clinical competence without the required standards |
| HOCl / disinfection | Product-specific regulatory, COSHH, training, efficacy, insurance, application and claims evidence |

## Immediate Production Order

### Days 1-2: identity and access

1. Appoint first and backup administrators.
2. Create individual One Login accounts.
3. Register UCM on Find a Tender.
4. Record the PPON.
5. Complete basic information and the connected-person register.
6. Establish the private evidence-library structure.

### Days 3-5: hard admission gates

1. Obtain current insurance schedules and broker gap analysis.
2. Ask the accountant for full accounts, management context and viability support.
3. Confirm VAT, tax and bank-onboarding evidence.
4. Review exclusions with a director and adviser where necessary.
5. Start Cyber Essentials.
6. Collect all current policies, RAMS, COSHH, SDS and training records.

### Days 6-8: operational proof

1. Finalise service catalogue and boundaries.
2. Build the training, equipment and insurance expiry registers.
3. Produce two approved case studies and reference permissions.
4. Produce sample KPI, quality-inspection and monthly-reporting outputs.
5. Finalise safeguarding, complaints, continuity and subcontractor controls.

### Days 9-10: commercial bid pack

1. Create the capability statement.
2. Build the method-answer and mobilisation libraries.
3. Complete the pricing and bid/no-bid models.
4. Build the carbon baseline and social-value evidence plan.
5. Generate a current CDP share code/file.
6. Complete the RM6264-specific readiness register.
7. Begin buyer portals one by one, recording the exact next action in Tender Control.

## Operating Rules

- One evidence item has one owner and one review date.
- No status becomes **Ready** until the source document has been checked.
- A certificate expiring during a likely contract award or mobilisation period is treated as a risk now.
- Every submission gets director approval before release.
- Never email highly sensitive data unless the buyer explicitly requires it and the channel is secure.
- Never reuse a method response without checking the new question and contract.
- Do not buy accreditations merely for appearance; buy them when a target route requires them and expected revenue justifies the cost.
- Record losses and clarification answers so each bid improves the next.
